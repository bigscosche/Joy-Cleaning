How Often Should You Schedule
House Cleaning?

There's no single right answer — but there are good ones for different kinds of households. Here's a practical guide to choosing a cleaning frequency that actually works for your home and your life.

Why Frequency Matters More Than You Think

Most people think about cleaning frequency in terms of how dirty their home gets. That's part of it — but the right frequency also determines how thorough each visit can be, how much your home maintains its condition over time, and ultimately what you pay per visit.

Here's the thing: a bi-weekly clean is easier and more thorough than a monthly clean, because less has accumulated in the two weeks between visits. A monthly clean is always starting from a worse baseline, which means more time and more elbow grease for the same hourly rate. If you feel like your home never gets properly clean despite regular service, frequency is often the issue — not the cleaner.

The Four Main Options

Weekly Cleaning

Best for: Families with young children, households with multiple pets, high-traffic homes, or anyone whose home gets genuinely messy in a week.

Weekly cleaning keeps your home at a consistently high standard. The visits are faster (because less builds up) and tend to be the most thorough relative to what needs to be done. If you're paying for weekly service, you should be coming home to a home that feels clean every single time — not just sometimes.

The tradeoff is cost — weekly service costs more per month than bi-weekly, even if the per-visit rate is lower. But for the right household, it's the only frequency that actually maintains the standard you want.

Bi-Weekly Cleaning

Best for: Most households — working couples, families with school-age kids, pet owners who want a clean home without the weekly cost.

Bi-weekly is our most popular schedule for a reason. Every two weeks is frequent enough that each visit is efficient and thorough, but not so frequent that it feels excessive for a well-maintained home. If you're tidy between visits — dishes done, basic clutter picked up — a bi-weekly clean keeps things at a genuinely good standard.

This is usually the best balance of quality, consistency, and cost for most Fairfield County households.

Monthly Cleaning

Best for: Singles or couples without kids or pets, part-time residents, second homes, or people who do a lot of day-to-day maintenance themselves and just need a professional reset monthly.

Monthly works well for the right household — but it's often not the right choice for busy families. By the time four weeks have passed, things have accumulated to the point where the visit takes longer, costs more, and still might not catch everything. If you're booking monthly and your home never quite gets to where you want it, consider going bi-weekly for a few months and see if it makes a difference.

As-Needed / One-Time

Best for: Move-outs, post-construction, seasonal deep cleans, or situations where recurring service isn't practical.

One-time visits are always available, but they tend to cost more per visit than a recurring plan. They're the right choice for specific situations — not as a long-term cleaning strategy. If you find yourself booking one-time visits frequently, you'd almost certainly save money (and get better results) on a recurring plan.

Factors That Should Influence Your Decision

Number of People in the Home

More people = more mess. A couple in a 2-bedroom home can often get away with bi-weekly service. A family of five in the same size home probably needs weekly. More people means surfaces get dirtier faster, bathrooms require more frequent attention, and the kitchen accumulates grease and spills more quickly.

Pets

Pets — especially dogs and cats that shed — significantly increase the frequency you need. Pet hair settles into every surface, odors accumulate in upholstery and rugs, and muddy paw prints happen regardless of how careful you are. Most households with one or more pets benefit from weekly or reliable bi-weekly service. Monthly rarely cuts it.

Allergies or Sensitivities

If anyone in your household has asthma, allergies, or sensitivities to dust and pet dander, more frequent professional cleaning genuinely helps. Dust mites, allergens, and particulates build up faster than you can see, and professional cleaning with the right products removes them more thoroughly than DIY cleaning typically does.

Your Own Maintenance Habits

Are you the type to clean up spills immediately, keep counters clear, and run the dishwasher daily? Or does the kitchen tend to accumulate over the course of a week? Be honest about this. There's no judgment — but your habits should inform your frequency. A neat household can often stretch to bi-weekly. A busy one usually can't.

Your Budget

This is a real constraint and there's no reason to pretend otherwise. If bi-weekly is out of reach right now, monthly is better than nothing — and you can always increase frequency later. The best cleaning plan is the one you'll actually stick to. See our pricing guide for Fairfield County for a realistic sense of what different options cost.

What We Recommend

For most households in Fairfield County — families, working couples, homes with pets — bi-weekly is the sweet spot. It's frequent enough to maintain a genuinely clean home, efficient enough that each visit covers everything well, and cost-effective compared to the weekly option.

We recommend starting with a deep clean to establish a baseline, then moving to bi-weekly maintenance. Every six to twelve months, schedule another deep clean to address what the regular visits can't reach — inside appliances, behind furniture, grout, and detailed baseboards.

If your household is particularly high-traffic, or you have young kids or multiple pets, go weekly. You'll feel the difference within the first month.

The Bottom Line

Weekly if your home gets genuinely messy in a week. Bi-weekly for most households — it's the right balance of quality and cost. Monthly if you're a neat household and mainly want a professional reset. And always start with a deep clean so the recurring service has a proper baseline to maintain.
