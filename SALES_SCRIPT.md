# Sales Script

Not a script you read line-by-line. A structure you internalize so the conversation naturally flows in the right direction every time.

Be a person. Not a pitch deck.

---

## Cold opener (phone or walk-in)

3 seconds to earn 30 more. Short, real, non-aggressive.

> "Hey, this is [name]. I'm not selling SEO — I build systems that bring local service businesses more booked jobs. Looked at your site and there's some real opportunity. Got 90 seconds?"

**If yes:**
> "Here's what I noticed: [1 concrete observation about their site — slow load, weak form, missing service page, thin area coverage]. Also, I saw you're not ranking for [obvious local search term they should own]. That's almost certainly leaking you jobs to [specific competitor they'd recognize]. I can show you a way to fix both in about two weeks. Worth a 20-min call?"

**If busy:**
> "Totally fair. Can I text you a 90-second Loom of what I'd build? No meeting, no pitch — just so you can see it."

## Email / DM opener

Subject line — **don't write "SEO" or "marketing"**. These work:
- *"[Their business name] — 3 local searches you're losing right now"*
- *"Noticed something about [their website]"*
- *"Quick idea for [their business] this quarter"*

Body (under 100 words):

> Hey [name] —
>
> Not a pitch. I build lead generation systems for local [service] businesses — the whole funnel from Google search to your phone ringing. Most sites in your space look like brochures.
>
> Looked at [their site]. Two things jumped out: [concrete observation 1] and [concrete observation 2]. Real revenue sitting on the table.
>
> If you want, I'll send a 2-minute video showing what this would look like. Free, no obligation.
>
> — [name]

## Discovery call (20–30 min)

Before pitching anything, understand the business.

### Questions to ask, in order
1. "Walk me through how a customer finds you today — what's the path?"
2. "How many leads come in per week? Where do they come from?"
3. "How many of those turn into booked jobs?"
4. "What's your average job size?"
5. "What happens when a lead comes in — who handles it, how fast?"
6. "What's the biggest bottleneck right now — lead flow, conversion, or operations?"
7. "If leads doubled tomorrow, could you handle it? What would break?"

You're not just gathering information. You're making them see the problem themselves. By question 7 they've done the math in their head.

### The pivot (after they answer)

> "OK, so the short version: you're doing about $[X] per month, average job is $[Y], you're at capacity on operations but under-leveraged on lead flow. That's exactly the business this system is built for."

---

## The pitch (three sentences, then shut up)

> "Here's what I'd build: a set of local pages targeting [their top 5 towns and 3 services], a lead form that pings your phone within seconds of someone submitting, and a dashboard showing you every lead, every job, and your profit this month. It replaces whatever site you have today and plugs into [Jobber/HouseCallPro/whatever they use] so nothing breaks. Two weeks start to finish, and you own everything."

Then stop talking. Let them ask.

## Common objections (honest answers)

### "How is this different from an SEO agency?"
> "SEO agencies sell rankings. I sell booked jobs. They bill you forever for the same work. I build a specific system, charge once to set it up, flat monthly for ongoing. The output is different too — their report is traffic; my report is leads and revenue."

### "How do I know it'll work?"
> "You don't, until it does. That's why I break it into stages: setup, 30-day checkpoint, 90-day review. If it's not generating real leads by day 90, you stop paying the monthly. I'd rather lose a client than drag one along."

### "Sounds expensive."
> "Let's do the math. Your average job is $[their number]. The Growth tier is $[price]. That means [N] booked jobs over the next year pays for everything. Most clients hit that in the first 4–8 weeks."

### "I already have a web person."
> "Cool — keep them for updates. I'm not building a site, I'm building a system. The two work together. Think of me as the revenue side and them as the design side."

### "I use HouseCallPro / Jobber already."
> "Perfect. Those run operations — scheduling, invoicing, payment. My system runs the front door — search, site, form, notifications. Your existing tools stay exactly as they are; this feeds them."

### "I need to think about it / talk to my partner."
> "Totally reasonable. One thing to ask yourselves: if we doubled lead flow for six months, what would it change? The biggest mistake people make is waiting for the perfect time. Lead flow compounds — the sooner you start, the bigger the curve."

### "Can you do it cheaper?"
> "The cheaper version is the Starter tier — gets you online with a working form, no ongoing fee. But it skips the SEO pages that actually drive leads. You'd essentially pay less to get less. I'd rather you start with Growth for three months and cancel if it doesn't work."

### "I'll get back to you."
> "Sure. One thing — I only take [N] new builds a month because of how involved they are. If you want to be in [this month]'s batch, I'd need a yes by [date]. No pressure — just don't want you to hear back and find I'm booked through [later month]."

### "Can you guarantee a number of leads?"
> "I can't guarantee leads, but I can guarantee the system — built, launched, working. What I can do is structure it so if you're on Performance you only pay per qualified lead delivered. That's the closest thing to a guarantee either of us will ever be honest about."

### "What if I hate it?"
> "Cancel any time after the minimum term. You keep the site, the domain, the database. Everything I build goes into your Supabase and your hosting. If we part ways, you walk away with a working asset."

## The close (offer two paths, not yes/no)

> "Two ways to start. Easiest: you sign today, I kick off Monday, site is live by [2 weeks from Monday]. Or: I send you a one-page preview with the homepage mocked up in your branding — no commitment — and you decide from there. Either works. What's easier for you?"

**If preview:** send within 48 hours. Follow up in 5 days with *"thoughts?"*

**If signup:** send a 1-page agreement, 50% invoice upfront, kickoff date on the calendar.

## After the sale (the referral loop)

This is where the real money lives.

| Day | What you do |
|---|---|
| Day 1 | Send intake form + kickoff invite |
| Day 7 | Send WIP preview ("here's your site so far, anything jumping out?") |
| Day 14 | Launch + training call |
| Day 21 | Check-in text: "any leads come through? Anything weird?" |
| Day 30 | Send stat snapshot: leads this month, response time, top pages |
| Day 45 | Ask for referral. "If you know 2 other [landscapers/cleaners/contractors] who'd benefit, I'll take $500 off their monthly." |
| Day 90 | Performance review — is Performance tier worth the upgrade? |

---

## What NOT to do

- Don't lead with "SEO." Let them say it first, then reframe.
- Don't send a 12-page proposal. A 1-page agreement closes more.
- Don't discount your setup fee to win a skeptical client — they'll be more skeptical after they pay.
- Don't promise a specific number of leads. Promise the system + a review window.
- Don't pitch the dashboard first. Pitch the outcome (more jobs, more revenue), then show the dashboard as proof you can measure it.
- Don't talk about yourself. Every sentence should be about their business.
- Don't "keep in touch" indefinitely. If they're not a yes in 2 weeks, they're a no. Move on.

---

## The only three things a prospect needs to believe

If these three things land, you close. If any one is missing, you don't.

1. **"This person understands my business."** — prove it with 2 specific observations about their site during the first 90 seconds.
2. **"This works, and I can measure it."** — show the dashboard. Show the Slack flow. Show the math.
3. **"This is worth more than it costs."** — do the lead value math out loud, using their own numbers.

Three beliefs. That's the whole job.
