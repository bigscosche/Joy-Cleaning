## How to price for a specific client (the math you show them)

Use this during the pitch. It's the closer.

1. "What's your average job worth?" → call it **$J**
2. "What % of leads turn into booked jobs?" → call it **C** (typically 40–70%)
3. Lead value = **$J × C**
4. Growth tier year one = $3,500 setup + $400/mo × 12 = **$8,300**
5. Break-even = $8,300 / (J × C) leads

### Example — cleaning business
- Average job: $175
- Conversion: 55%
- Lead value: $96
- Break-even: **87 leads over 12 months** — 7.25 per month

Competent local SEO build typically delivers 15–30 qualified leads per month by month 3. So Growth tier pays for itself inside 4 months; everything after is profit.

### Example — roofing business
- Average job: $1,200
- Conversion: 30%
- Lead value: $360
- Break-even: **23 leads over 12 months** — less than 2 per month

Roofing sells itself. Any system that produces even one extra lead a month is a home run.

## How to frame the cost

Never compare your price to "other marketing tools." Always compare it to:

- The cost of one booked job they'd have missed
- The cost of one full-time employee (even part-time is $1,500+/month)
- The cost of staying invisible on Google for another year

Example line: *"The monthly fee is less than one oil change per week. One extra booked job a month covers it four times over."*
